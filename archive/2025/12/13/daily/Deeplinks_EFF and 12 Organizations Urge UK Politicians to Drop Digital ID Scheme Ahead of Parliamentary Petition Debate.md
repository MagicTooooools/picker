The UK Parliament convened earlier this week to debate a [petition](https://petition.parliament.uk/petitions/730194) signed by 2.9 million people calling for an end to the [government’s plans](https://www.gov.uk/government/news/new-digital-id-scheme-to-be-rolled-out-across-uk) to roll out a national digital ID. Ahead of that debate, EFF and 12 other civil society organizations [wrote to politicians in the country](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/NGO-Joint-Digital-ID-Debate-Briefing.pdf) urging MPs to reject the Labour government’s newly announced digital ID proposal.

The UK’s Prime Minister Keir Starmer [pitched the scheme](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clyl3lzzed2o) as a way to “cut the [faff](https://www.lexicallab.com/2017/11/word-of-the-day-faff/)” in proving people’s identities by creating a virtual ID on personal devices with information like names, date of birth, nationality, photo, and residency status to verify their right to live and work in the country.

But the case for digital identification has not been made.
