## An introduction to workflow and process automation with n8n and Camunda

Andrea Dainese

December 07, 2025

[Learning paths](/categories/learning-paths/ "All posts under Learning paths"),
[Automation](/categories/automation/ "All posts under Automation")

[![Post cover](/images/categories/learning-paths.webp)](/images/categories/learning-paths.webp)

In a corporate environment, the natural evolution of network automation is to plug itself directly into business processes, speeding them up and making them more cost-effective.

Over the past few years, I’ve often come across
[Camunda](https://camunda.com/)
. Camunda is an open-source platform for business process automation and orchestration that helps coordinate complex workflows involving people, systems, and devices.

In other words, Camunda lets us design business processes, orchestrate them, automate them, and monitor them:

[![Patreon Image](/blog/2025/12/07/an-introduction-to-workflow-and-process-automation-with-n8n-and-camunda/80af2ed97c86f8b191d1800862d67df5.webp)](/blog/2025/12/07/an-introduction-to-workflow-and-process-automation-with-n8n-and-camunda/80af2ed97c86f8b191d1800862d67df5.webp)

Camunda is built to handle complex, long-running processes represented using the BPMN (Business Process Model and Notation) standard. Different processes can communicate with each other by passing variables across workflows.

[the post on Patreon](https://www.patreon.com/posts/144206553)
