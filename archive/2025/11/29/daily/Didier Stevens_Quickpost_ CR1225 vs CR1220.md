### Quickpost: CR1225 vs CR1220

Filed under: [Hardware](https://blog.didierstevens.com/category/hardware/),[Quickpost](https://blog.didierstevens.com/category/quickpost/) — Didier Stevens @ 0:00

I had to replace a [button cell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Button_cell), a CR1225, but I only had a CR1220.

![](https://blog.didierstevens.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/20251120-105312.png)

So I just used that CR1220 in stead. This works, because a CR1220 and CR1225 differ in mechanical properties (dimension), but not in electrical properties (voltage).

Both cells have a nominal voltage of 3 Volts.

CR1220 [means the following](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Button_cell#Package_size):

* C: Lithium battery
* R: Round
* 12: 12mm diameter
* 20: 2,0mm thickness

The difference between a CR1220 and CR1225 is the thickness: a CR1220 is 0,5mm slimmer than a CR1225. So a CR1220 fits in a CR1225 holder without problem.
