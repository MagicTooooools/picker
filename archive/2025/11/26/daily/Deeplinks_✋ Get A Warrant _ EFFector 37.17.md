Even with the holidays coming up, the digital rights news doesn't stop. Thankfully, EFF is here to keep you up-to-date with our EFFector newsletter!

In [our latest issue](https://www.eff.org/effector/37/17), we’re explaining why politicians latest [attempts to ban VPNs](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/11/lawmakers-want-ban-vpns-and-they-have-no-idea-what-theyre-doing) is a terrible idea; asking supporters to file public comments opposing new rules that would [make bad patents untouchable](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/11/patent-office-about-make-bad-patents-untouchable); and sharing a privacy victory—Sacramento is forced to [end its dragnet surveillance program](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/11/victory-court-end-dragnet-electricity-surveillance-program-sacramento) of power meter data.

Prefer to listen in? Check out our audio companion, where EFF Surveillance Litigation Director Andrew Crocker explains our new lawsuit [challenging the warrantless mass surveillance](https://www.eff.org/press/releases/lawsuit-challenges-san-joses-warrantless-alpr-mass-surveillance) of drivers in San Jose. Catch the conversation on [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T3dq9E-RZ-E) or the [Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/37.17).

Since 1990 EFF has [published EFFector](https://eff.org/effector) to help keep readers on the bleeding edge of their digital rights. We know that the intersection of technology, civil liberties, human rights, and the law can be complicated, so EFFector is a great way to stay on top of things. The newsletter is chock full of links to updates, announcements, blog posts, and other stories to help keep readers—and listeners—up to date on the movement to protect online privacy and free expression.

Thank you to the supporters around the world who make our work possible! If you're not a member yet, [join EFF today](https://eff.org/effect) to help us fight for a brighter digital future.
