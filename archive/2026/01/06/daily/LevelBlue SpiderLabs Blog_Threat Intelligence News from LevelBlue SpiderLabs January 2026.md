**MongoBleed: Critical MongoDB Vulnerability Under Active Exploitation**

On December 19, 2025, a severe vulnerability in MongoDB known as **MongoBleed (**[**CVE-2025-14847**](https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2025-14847)**)** was disclosed, impacting most MongoDB deployments. The flaw, rated **CVSSv4 8.7**, allows unauthenticated attackers to leak uninitialized heap memory, exposing sensitive data such as passwords and API keys. Public exploits appeared on December 25, and by December 28, widespread exploitation was confirmed. Despite urgent patching efforts, scans on December 30 revealed that nearly **70% of publicly accessible instances remained vulnerable**, leaving thousands of organizations exposed.
